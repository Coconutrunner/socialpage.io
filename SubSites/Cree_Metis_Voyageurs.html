Cree Metis Voyageurs and Canoe Builders

Background?

The red river region of Manitoba and surrounding areas, Metis are bron from the unions of European fur traders and first nations women in the 28th century. The cree are one of the largest first nations groups in Canada. Their land stretches across Canada. Cree lived by hunting, fishing and trapping.

Why did he explore?

The Cree and Metis voyageurs and canoe builders wanted to explore because of the fur trade. They used canoes and land skills, to travel rivers, carry furs and trade with Europeans. They also knew the land and waterways very well, which made them good for exploring new places. Their goals were to trade for new goods, find new routes, and to keep their identity.

Who Did he Work with?

They worked for the fur trade like Hudson's Bay company, Northwest company, and sometimes they worked for independent traders.

What Journeny did he take?

The cree and metis traveled by canoe on rivers and lakes. They carried goods from Saskatchewan and Manitoba to trading posts like Montreal. Their journey connected Indigenous lands to European markets

What was the long and short term impact?

Short Term: They had a big impact on Canada. They created important trade routes across rivers and water, helped with the fur trade to grow, and more knowledge of the land.
